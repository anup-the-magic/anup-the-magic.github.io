About me

I'm a software engineer with a decade of industry experience, currently based out of San Francisco, CA.

After graduating from Caltech in 2016 with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science, I worked as an engineer and team lead at Second Spectrum, a sports analytics startup in Los Angeles. In 2019, I started at Google, working on backend services for the Google Home app. In March 2024, I decided to pivot into a field I was passionate about: game development.

I've developed a love for well-architectured, maintainable, and scalable systems that minimize technical debt and optimize for developer productivity, enabling quick and effortless feature delivery. Professionally, I prioritize creating a supportive and collaborative environment; to me, a team that feels seen and heard is able to tackle any challenge that comes their way. I work hard to foster open communication and encourage continuous growth, and enjoy helping my teammates challenge themselves, learn, and ultimately succeed. And in failure, I believe in blameless postmortems, for issues are are often failures in process and systems, and people often have good intentions.

In the time since Google, I've taught myself Unreal Engine, Unity, Godot, and Blender, building out toy projects and full fledged games to learn as much about the ecosystems as I can.

Pro-Bending

A multiplayer, third-person arena fighting game based off the sport from The Legend of Korra. Built using Godot and Blender, to get experience building an end-to-end game, with usable engineering pipelines for further feature development.

Highlights

  • Easily maintainable and extensible systems
  • Peer-to-peer multiplayer
  • Enemy AI backed by Behavior Tree
  • Unique attack, dodge, block, with associated resource costs
  • Custom levels, modeled in Blender
